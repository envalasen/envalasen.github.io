    My passion for videogames has been inherent in my personality from a very young age.
    Not only have I played games since I was a kid, but I have also played electronic-sports since I was 12 years old, being both the captain and in-game leader for several teams. This is how I would say I have delevoped most of my soft skils, such as communication, leadership and adaptability.

    While I was studying my degree in Computer Engineering and Business Administration, I had the chance of working as a backend developer in Ford Credit Spain, where I could forge my skills in devolping microservices in Java, using frameworks like SpringBoot or tools such as Gradle and RabbitMQ.
    I also got to use CI/CD tools like Jenkins and learned some basic software developing principles (mainly SOLID principles), which I would later use on the development of the videogames I was involved in.

    For my Computer Engineering Bachelor's Thesis, I developed my first game in Unity (called Typing Hero, which you can see below), and, for the Business Administration Bachelor's Thesis, I studied the Marketing Plan for such a game. This is how I found out my keenness on developing videogames, hence I enrolled in a Master's Degree in Game Programming, where I had the opportunity to develop two more games, Aard and Wyzz: The Rise of Minions and Pathogenesis, in Unreal Engine 5.2 and Unity, respectively.
